Assignment Instructions
Assignment 1.1 — On Location Camera Composition

Capture five different shot types on location — not in a studio. All footage must be recorded horizontally. For each shot, upload your frame and write a minimum of 50 words explaining what makes it that shot type.

The Five Shots
Wide Shot
Establishes the full location. The viewer should immediately understand where the scene takes place — the environment is the subject.
Full Shot
Shows a subject from head to toe within the environment. Both the person and their surroundings are clearly visible in the frame.
Medium Shot
Frames the subject from the waist up. Balances the subject's body language and expression with some environmental context.
Close Up
Fills the frame with the subject's face or a key detail. The background recedes — the subject becomes the sole focus.
Extreme Close Up
Isolates a single specific detail — an eye, a hand, a texture, a prop. Reveals something the wider shots cannot show.
